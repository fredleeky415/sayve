# Sayve Manifesto & Mechanics

Last updated: 2026-07-09

## 1. Executive Summary

Sayve is not a bookkeeping app.

Sayve is an AI Native Family Financial Memory.

The core idea is simple:

> A family should not need to manage bookkeeping software.
> They should only need to tell Sayve what happened.

Traditional personal finance tools ask users to become data clerks. They require people to open an app, choose a category, enter a merchant, enter an amount, choose a date, save a transaction, and later maintain the data.

Sayve takes the opposite direction.

Sayve treats every input as a memory, not as a form submission. A user can type one sentence, speak one line, or upload one receipt. The system captures the raw input, lets AI interpret it, stores the facts, updates household context, connects related memories, and makes the result queryable through conversation, dashboard, timeline, and future views.

The product belief is:

> AI should adapt to the user's life flow.
> The user should not adapt to an app's data-entry flow.

V1 focuses on Family Financial Memory. Long term, the same Memory Engine can support Home Memory, Warranty Memory, Insurance Memory, Car Memory, Medical Memory, and Document Memory.

## 2. Why This Exists

Most people do not dislike knowing where their money goes.

They dislike the work required to maintain the record.

The market already has many strong bookkeeping and expense-tracking products. Their common assumption is that users will create and maintain structured financial data.

That assumption breaks down for normal families.

In real life, family spending is messy:

- One partner pays sometimes, the other pays sometimes.
- Some spending is personal, but most household spending is shared.
- A receipt may arrive after a voice note.
- The user may say "Cut Netflix" instead of giving a clean accounting instruction.
- Household context changes: moving home, new job, child stage, cancelled subscriptions, sold car, new helper, insurance renewal.
- Users want to ask "why did we spend more?" rather than manually inspect categories.

Traditional bookkeeping apps make these messy life events fit into transaction rows.

Sayve lets the messy event enter as-is, then lets the Memory Engine organize it.

## 3. Product Manifesto

### 3.1 Memory Before Bookkeeping

Bookkeeping is a data structure.

Memory is a product experience.

A transaction database can say:

> Netflix, HKD 88, July 2026.

A family financial memory should also understand:

> Netflix was a recurring household subscription.
> The user later said it was cancelled.
> Future Netflix charges should no longer be expected.
> If Netflix appears again, it may be worth pointing out.

This is the difference between recording money and understanding household life.

### 3.2 Capture First

The first job of Sayve is to receive.

The user should be able to drop a thought into Sayve as naturally as sending a message:

- "今日大家樂食飯 300."
- "今個月開始唔需要再畀 Netflix."
- Receipt photo.
- Voice note.
- "上個月食飯用咗幾多?"

Sayve should not force the user to decide too early whether something is an expense, a correction, a context update, a question, a receipt, or a subscription change.

The system can decide.

### 3.3 Every Tap Is A Tax

Every extra tap is a tax on habit formation.

If Sayve asks too many questions, users will stop using it.

The correct default is:

- High confidence: remember silently.
- Medium confidence: remember now, mark for later review.
- Low confidence, low impact: keep the capture, review later.
- Low confidence, high impact: ask one minimal question.

Sayve should not become a chatty accountant.

### 3.4 Facts Are Sacred, Context Evolves

Sayve separates two things:

Facts are things that happened.

Context is what the household currently understands to be true.

Example:

- Fact: 2026-07-08, Netflix, HKD 88.
- Context: Netflix has been cancelled from August 2026.

If the user says "Cut 咗 Netflix", Sayve should not delete previous Netflix spending. Historical facts remain. The correct action is to update household context and change future expectations.

### 3.5 One Household, One Memory

Sayve is family-first, not personal-first.

Each family member logs in with their own account, but they write into the same household memory.

This matters because a family does not experience finance as two separate ledgers. The household wants to know:

- How much did we spend?
- What changed this month?
- Which fixed costs are still active?
- Which spending is shared?
- Which spending belongs to one person personally?

Audit attribution and spending ownership are different.

`createdBy` means who told Sayve the information. It does not always mean who paid.

If the user does not clearly say "我自己", "太太自己", or another personal ownership phrase, Sayve defaults the spending to shared household spending.

## 4. What Sayve Is Not

Sayve is not:

- A prettier transaction form.
- A dashboard-first budgeting app.
- A spreadsheet replacement only.
- A chatbot that guesses without evidence.
- A receipt OCR tool with categories added.
- A personal ledger for one person only.

Sayve is:

- A low-friction capture interface.
- A household-level memory system.
- A reasoning layer over financial facts and household context.
- A conversation interface for understanding money.
- A system that improves as the family uses and corrects it.

## 5. Core User Experience

The product has three user-facing surfaces.

### 5.1 Home: Tell Sayve One Thing

Home is not a dashboard.

Home is not a report.

Home is a lightweight capture surface.

The user sees:

> 跟 Sayve 說一件事

They can:

- Type one line.
- Take a receipt photo.
- Record a short voice note.

The interaction should feel like dropping something into an inbox.

After capture, Sayve can simply respond:

> Sayved.

This is important. The success feeling is not "I filled a form." The success feeling is "I told Sayve, and I can move on."

### 5.2 Ask: Conversation With Memory

Ask is for questions and analysis.

Examples:

- "上個月食飯用咗幾多?"
- "點解今個月支出高咗?"
- "最近咖啡係咪太多?"
- "固定開支有咩?"
- "Netflix 仲係咪 active?"
- "我需要擔心嗎?"

Sayve should answer briefly and clearly.

The AI personality is a Financial Memory Companion:

- Concise.
- Calm.
- Evidence-based.
- Not preachy.
- Not verbose like a general chatbot.
- Not an accountant asking the user to fill missing fields.

The system should retrieve relevant structured data first, then use AI to explain.

The intended flow is:

```text
User question
  -> Intent detection
  -> SQL / structured retrieval
  -> Relevant memory/context retrieval
  -> AI explanation
  -> Short answer with source references where useful
```

Sayve should not send the entire memory history into the AI model every time.

### 5.3 Dashboard: A View Over Memory

Dashboard is useful, but it is not the product core.

Dashboard is one way to read memory.

It should show:

- Monthly income.
- Monthly spending.
- Profit/loss line.
- Spending by category.
- Daily spending calendar.
- Monthly list of memory facts.
- Custom household categories.
- Category trend over the year.

Dashboard should be generated from the Memory Engine, not maintained as separate business logic.

The user should be able to understand the month at a glance, but they should not need to live inside the dashboard.

## 6. Memory Engine Mechanics

The Memory Engine is the core product asset.

OpenAI is not the memory.

OpenAI is the reasoning engine.

Sayve owns the memory.

This separation is important because it allows:

- Persistent memory independent of any model provider.
- Model upgrades.
- Re-interpretation of old captures.
- Auditability.
- Future mobile app handoff.
- Future domains beyond finance.

### 6.1 Capture Layer

The Capture Layer stores what the user originally gave Sayve.

Examples:

- Raw text.
- Voice transcript.
- Receipt image reference.
- Uploaded file metadata.
- Timestamp.
- Member who captured it.
- Device/source metadata.

The raw capture is important because AI interpretation can improve later.

If future AI models become better, Sayve can reprocess old captures without asking the user to re-enter them.

### 6.2 Interpretation Layer

The Interpretation Layer stores what AI believed the capture meant at a point in time.

It includes:

- Intent.
- Structured output.
- Confidence.
- Model used.
- Prompt version.
- Reasoning summary.
- Cost and latency telemetry.
- Whether the result was auto-confirmed, review-later, or required user input.

Interpretations are versioned.

This means Sayve can say:

> At the time, this was understood as Dining.
> Later, after learning the user's custom category, it was reclassified as Family Meal.

### 6.3 Fact Layer

Facts represent what happened.

For financial memory, facts can include:

- Date.
- Amount.
- Currency.
- Merchant.
- Category.
- Direction: income or expense.
- Ownership scope: shared or member.
- Source references.

Facts are append-only in normal product behavior.

If AI was wrong, Sayve should add a revision, correction, or new interpretation. It should not quietly overwrite history.

Privacy deletion is different. If a user asks for legal/privacy deletion, Sayve must support true redaction or deletion through a separate privacy path.

### 6.4 Context Layer

Context represents the current household state.

Examples:

- Netflix cancelled.
- Child no longer needs formula.
- Car sold.
- Moved home.
- New job.
- Helper salary changed.
- Mortgage started.
- School fee period changed.

Context is not a transaction.

Context changes how future facts are interpreted.

If Netflix is marked cancelled, future Netflix charges become unusual. If a baby no longer needs formula, future formula spending may be notable.

### 6.5 Relationship Layer

Relationships connect memories.

Examples:

- Receipt supports a voice capture.
- Two captures describe the same event.
- Merchant alias maps ParknShop, PNS, and 百佳.
- A fact conflicts with current household context.
- A correction replaces a previous interpretation.
- An insight references several facts.

This is where Sayve becomes more than a transaction table.

## 7. AI Behaviour Mechanics

### 7.1 Capture Decision Flow

When a capture arrives, Sayve should classify it into one of several intents:

- Financial event.
- Income event.
- Subscription or recurring event.
- Household context update.
- Question.
- Correction.
- Receipt support.
- Ambiguous capture.

Then it decides how much friction is appropriate.

High confidence:

- Create or update memory.
- Auto-confirm.
- Show minimal response.

Medium confidence:

- Create memory.
- Mark `review_later`.
- Do not interrupt the user immediately.

Low confidence:

- If low impact, keep it for later review.
- If high impact, ask one minimal question.

High impact includes:

- Deleting or redacting data.
- Merging many records.
- Changing household context in a way that affects future expectations.
- Large unusual amounts.
- Personal ownership assignment with weak evidence.

### 7.2 Merge Logic

Sayve must avoid duplicate memories.

Example:

1. User says: "今日食飯 300."
2. Later uploads receipt: HKD 298.5.

Traditional apps may create two transactions.

Sayve should understand that these likely refer to the same event.

Merge signals include:

- Time proximity.
- Amount proximity.
- Merchant.
- Category.
- Location.
- Household member.
- Semantic similarity.
- Receipt confidence.

The merge should not delete either capture.

It should create a relationship:

> This receipt supports the same memory as the voice/text capture.

### 7.3 Category Learning

Sayve should ship with default categories, but households must be able to create their own.

Default categories are useful for starting:

- Dining.
- Groceries.
- Transport.
- Housing.
- Utilities.
- Health and Insurance.
- Children.
- Subscriptions.
- Salary.
- Investment Income.

Custom categories are more important over time.

When the user creates a category, it teaches Sayve how the family thinks.

Example:

- Default category: Children.
- Custom category: BB Supplies.
- Custom category: Family Meal.
- Custom category: Helper.
- Custom category: School.

Custom categories should influence future AI classification.

The category system is not just UI filtering. It is household-specific learning.

### 7.4 Short Answers To Save Tokens

Sayve should not answer like a full ChatGPT conversation unless needed.

Default answers should be short:

- Direct number.
- One sentence explanation.
- One notable reason.
- Source count or confidence if useful.

Example:

> 上個月食飯約 HKD 4,280。比前一個月高約 18%，主要多咗兩次家庭聚餐。

Only expand if the user asks why, asks for breakdown, or asks whether to worry.

This improves:

- Speed.
- Cost.
- User trust.
- Product personality.

## 8. Data Mechanics

### 8.1 Single Source Of Truth

The Financial Memory is the source of truth.

Everything else is a view:

- Dashboard.
- Timeline.
- Conversation answer.
- Insights.
- Reports.
- Future app widgets.

Sayve should avoid creating separate product logic for each view.

### 8.2 Database As Projection

The database is not the product starting point.

The database stores projections of the Memory Engine.

Core objects include:

- Households.
- Household members.
- Captures.
- Memory objects.
- Interpretations.
- Facts.
- Context.
- Relationships.
- Revisions.
- Insights.
- Conversation messages.
- AI jobs and telemetry.

This structure allows Sayve to behave like memory rather than a spreadsheet.

### 8.3 Query Mechanics

For clear numerical questions, Sayve should use structured retrieval.

Example:

> 上個月食飯用咗幾多?

This should be answered by querying structured facts:

- Date range.
- Category.
- Direction.
- Amount.
- Household id.

AI should then phrase the answer, not calculate from scratch.

For fuzzy questions, Sayve combines structured data with context and relevant memories.

Example:

> 點解今個月貴咗?

Sayve should compare:

- Current month vs previous month.
- Category deltas.
- Unusual one-off events.
- Recurring changes.
- Household context changes.

The AI explains the reason using retrieved evidence.

### 8.4 Long-Term Memory Performance

Sayve should not become slow after three years of use.

The system should not send all historical records into AI.

Instead:

- Store structured facts for fast SQL queries.
- Store context separately for current household state.
- Store relationships for known connections.
- Retrieve only relevant records for each question.
- Summarize older periods when appropriate.
- Use dashboard/list templates for standard views without AI calls.

Dashboard and monthly lists do not need AI every time. They should be generated from structured memory data.

AI is used for interpretation, explanation, ambiguity handling, insight generation, and conversation.

Key modules:

- AI cost analytics.

- Household analytics.

- Cost per household.

## 10. Cost Mechanics

Sayve's cost model must be designed from the beginning.

The app should not call the strongest AI model for every action.

Suggested model strategy:

- Low-cost model for capture extraction.
- Vision model only when receipt image needs reading.
- Speech-to-text model only for audio.
- Moderate reasoning model for conversation.
- Higher model only for complex analysis or low-confidence escalation.

Standard dashboard, monthly list, and charts should not require AI calls.

Cost guardrails:

- Monthly capture limits during prototype/beta.
- Receipt upload byte limit.
- Voice upload byte limit.
- Speech-to-text size/type guardrails.
- Receipt vision size/type guardrails.
- Founder cost telemetry for every AI call.
- Public readiness checks before launch.

The business must know:

- Average AI cost per active household.
- Average AI cost per memory.
- Heavy-user distribution.
- Whether pricing covers support, marketing, infra, and margin.

## 11. Commercial Hypothesis

Sayve is not selling "record keeping".

Sayve is selling:

> Peace of mind that the family financial memory is being remembered, organized, and explainable.

The paying customer is likely not the person who loves bookkeeping.

The paying customer is the family that wants clarity without admin work.

Possible early adopter profiles:

- Couples managing shared household spending.
- Families with children and many recurring expenses.
- People who already keep loose records in notes, WhatsApp, receipts, or spreadsheets.
- Households that have tried budgeting apps but stopped because the input burden was too high.
- Founders/freelancers who mix home and personal finance but want a family view.

Important pricing reality:

People who are careful about money may be price-sensitive.

Therefore Sayve must create value beyond "saving a few dollars":

- Reducing mental load.
- Preventing forgotten subscriptions.
- Helping couples share one financial picture.
- Explaining why spending changed.
- Making annual review/tax/insurance/warranty/document extensions possible later.

## 14. Key Risks

### 14.1 Habit Risk

If capture feels like work, the product fails.

Mitigation:

- Home stays extremely light.
- Sayved confirmation.
- Ask fewer questions.
- Review later instead of interrupting.

### 14.2 AI Accuracy Risk

If AI misclassifies too often, trust breaks.

Mitigation:

- Confidence thresholds.
- Review-later state.
- Revision history.
- Founder Console quality metrics.
- User correction flow that teaches Sayve.

### 14.3 Cost Risk

If every capture or question becomes expensive, margin disappears.

Mitigation:

- Use structured queries before AI.
- Keep answers short.
- Use cheaper models for extraction.
- Avoid AI for dashboard templates.
- Track cost per household.

### 14.4 Privacy Risk

Financial data is sensitive.

Mitigation:

- Household auth.
- Role-based access.
- Private media storage.
- No-store/noindex responses.
- Redaction path.
- Avoid direct browser writes to protected memory snapshots.

### 14.5 Positioning Risk

Users may think Sayve is "another bookkeeping app".

Mitigation:

- Product language must avoid "add transaction" as the main frame.
- Use "tell Sayve", "memory", "understood as", "Sayved".
- Conversation and capture must be the first impression, not dashboard.
